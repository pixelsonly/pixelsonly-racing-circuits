# Mobility Resort Motegi

Mobility Resort Motegi opened in 1997 in the hills of Motegi, Tochigi
Prefecture, built by Honda as a dual-circuit venue: a 1.5-mile oval Super
Speedway and the ~4.8 km road course recorded here. Known until March 2022 as
Twin Ring Motegi (the "twin ring" being the oval and the road course side by
side), the facility was renamed Mobility Resort Motegi on its 25th anniversary.
The road course runs clockwise over 14 turns (2.983 mi / 4.801 km) and is
exceptionally flat by Japanese standards, with only about 30 m of elevation
change across the lap.

## A stop-and-go circuit

Motegi is the textbook "stop-and-go" track: short straights firing into heavy
braking zones and a string of slow, near-hairpin corners, rewarding traction
and braking stability over flowing momentum. The opening right-hand pair
(Turns 1 and 2) is wide and fast, but the lap quickly settles into its rhythm of
hard stops and tight exits. The fast 130R sweep (Turn 6) and the third-gear
S-Curves (Turns 7-8) are the rare flowing relief before the slowest section of
the circuit.

## The Hairpin and Victory Corner

The first-gear Hairpin (Turn 10) is the heart of the lap: its exit feeds the
signature stretch where the road course dips through a tunnel beneath the oval's
back straight before opening onto the long back straightaway and a hard-braking
90-degree right-hander (Turn 11). The lap closes through two left-handers into
Victory Corner (Turn 14), a second-gear right-hander that is the last real
passing chance before the line, and the corner that has decided countless Super
Formula, Super GT and MotoGP finishes.
