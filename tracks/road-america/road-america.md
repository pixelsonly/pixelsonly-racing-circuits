# Road America

Road America opened in 1955 on rolling glacial moraine near Elkhart Lake,
Wisconsin, built by civil engineer Clif Tufte after Wisconsin banned the public
road races that had run through the village since 1950. The Full Course measures
4.048 mi (6.515 km) over fourteen turns, with roughly 45 m (150 ft) of elevation
change, and is one of the few major road circuits still racing essentially its
original 1950s configuration. Its scale and parkland setting earned it the
nickname "America's National Park of Speed."

## The Kink

The circuit's identity is its signature corner: The Kink at Turn 11, a blind,
near-flat right-hand bend on the back stretch taken at close to top speed with
almost no margin for error. It is the corner Road America is most recognized for,
and a temporary chicane has at times been installed ahead of it to slow
motorcycles and certain car classes.

## The Carousel and Canada Corner

The long, sweeping right-hand Carousel (Turns 9–10) rewards a patient,
throttle-feathering line, while Canada Corner (Turn 12) at the end of the back
stretch is a heavy-braking right-hander and one of the track's classic
late-braking passing spots. The closing sequence drops through the Thunder
Valley dip and climbs Bill Mitchell Bend (Turn 13) before the final right-hander
releases the cars onto the long start/finish straight.
